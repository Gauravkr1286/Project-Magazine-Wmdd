Virat Kohli’s unique century against Australia

By Cricket Historian | December 2, 2024

In a thrilling display of cricket, Virat Kohli achieved a historic milestone... Kohli is set to join Tendulkar as the only players to have played 100 or more matches against Australia. This milestone places Kohli in elite company. Behind Tendulkar, the list includes West Indies' Desmond Haynes (97 matches), Vivian Richards (88 matches) and India's MS Dhoni (91 matches).

While fans eagerly await a century from Virat Kohli's bat, IND vs AUS 3rd Test in Gabba Test will see him achieve a different kind of century. This will be Virat Kohli's 100th match against the Australian cricket team. Over 99 games so far, Kohli has amassed an impressive 5,326 runs at an average of 50.24, including 27 Tests, 49 ODIs, and 23 T20Is. Only Sachin Tendulkar has played more matches against Australia, appearing in 110 games where he scored 6,707 runs at an average of 49.68. Kohli is set to join Tendulkar as the only players to have played 100 or more matches against Australia. This milestone places Kohli in elite company. Behind Tendulkar, the list includes West Indies' Desmond Haynes (97 matches), Vivian Richards (88 matches) and India's MS Dhoni (91 matches).

MS Dhoni’s 2011 World Cup winning six eternalised at Mumbai’s Wankhede stadium

By Cricket Historian | December 2, 2024

This spot at Wankhede Stadium would be auctioned by Mumbai Cricket Association (MCA) and the funds collected would be used to sponsor the emerging players.

In 2011, MS Dhoni hit the winning runs at Mumbai’s Wankhede Stadium, bringing the World Cup home after a wait of 28 years. The spot where the ball rested when Dhoni struck six off former Sri Lankan bowler Nuwan Kulasekara has been given a special touch for fans. The official X handle of Mumbai Cricket Association (MCA) shared a picture of the memorial named ‘World Cup 2011 Victory Memorial stand’. “The two seats where MS Dhoni’s 2011 ODI World Cup winning six landed at the Wankhede Stadium will forever be symbolic to every cricket fan,” wrote the Mumbai Cricket Association (MCA) while sharing a picture on X. The seats bearing the numbers J 282-286, nestled in the fifth row of the pavilion, are now adorned with two replicas of World Cup trophies. It even has a photograph of Team India posing with the 2011 trophy.
